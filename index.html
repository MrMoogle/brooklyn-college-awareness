About Us

We as a nation suffer from a college attainment gap. We also suffer from a college attainment gap, as high school graduates from the wealthiest families in our nation are almost certain to continue on to higher education, while just over half of our high school graduates in the poorest quarter of families attend college. And while more than half of college students graduate within six years, the completion rate for low-income students is around 25 percent. Every Spring and Fall Break, we lead a weeklong community service trip to Brooklyn, New York. There, we work with high school students, many of whom are first generation college applicants or come from disadvantaged backgrounds. Our core mission is to help them achieve brighter futures by encouraging and inspiring them to continue their education beyond high school.

Only one in 10 students from low-income communities graduate from college. This Breakout trip to New York City will be a chance to witness inner-city public education in traditional and charter schools and learn about education reform all the way from the policy level to neighborhoods in Brooklyn. This trip will expose participants to the minority achievement gap, the school choice movement and sources of opposition, methods of evaluating school performance, mayoral control of school districts, and the role community organizing and parent activism play in the education reform movement.

Frequently Asked Questions

I. What are you looking for in applicants?

We love enthusiastic, passionate applicants who we believe can strongly connect with the students that we work with. Prior experience with teaching and community service is also a huge plus.

II. How many applicants do you usually end up accepting?

For our fall trip, we usually take between 40% - 60% of those who apply. A lot less people apply in the spring so for our spring trip, we usually take between 90% - 100%. We give precedence to past trip participants and applicants who previously applied but did not get in.

III. What does the $50 trip fee cover?

This fee will cover all food, accomodation, and transportation expenses for an entire week in New York City. We're extremely fortunate to be funded by Princeton ReachOut, an alumni organization dedicated to global community service.
